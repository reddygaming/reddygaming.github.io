FPS Optimization Guide

Maximize your Frames Per Second (FPS) in Fortnite to ensure a smooth and responsive gaming experience. A higher FPS allows you to react faster and gives you a significant competitive advantage.

1. In-Game Settings

Start with the in-game settings. Lowering these can provide a massive boost to your FPS without a significant visual impact.

  • Display Mode: Fullscreen is essential for the best performance.
  • Resolution: Keep this at your monitor's native resolution.
  • Frame Rate Limit: Set this to a value higher than your monitor's refresh rate (e.g., 144Hz monitor should be set to 160 FPS or uncapped).
  • Graphics Quality Presets: Set to 'Low' or 'Performance'.
  • 3D Resolution: Keep at 100% if possible. Lowering this can improve FPS but will make your game look blurry.
  • View Distance: 'Near' or 'Medium' is usually sufficient.
  • Textures: 'Low' or 'Medium'.
  • Effects & Post Processing: 'Low' or 'Off'.
  • Show FPS: Enable this to monitor your performance.

2. Windows Settings

Optimizing your operating system can free up valuable resources for your game.

  • Game Mode: Turn this ON in Windows Settings.
  • Background Apps: Turn OFF "Let apps run in the background".
  • Power Plan: Set to 'High Performance' in Power & sleep settings.
  • Startup Apps: Disable unnecessary programs from running when you start your PC.
  • Graphics Settings: Add Fortnite to this list and set it to 'High performance'.

3. Graphics Card Drivers & Settings

Outdated drivers can cause performance issues and instability. Keeping them updated is crucial.

  • Update Drivers: Regularly update your NVIDIA, AMD, or Intel graphics drivers.
  • NVIDIA Control Panel Settings:
    • Adjust image settings with preview: Use the advanced 3D image settings.
    • Manage 3D settings: Set 'Power management mode' to 'Prefer maximum performance'.

4. Final Tips

  • Close Other Programs: Close any browser tabs, Discord, or other applications you aren't actively using.
  • Overclocking: If you're comfortable with it, safely overclocking your GPU and CPU can provide a performance boost.
  • Upgrade Hardware: If you have an older system, the most significant FPS increase will come from upgrading your CPU, GPU, or RAM.
